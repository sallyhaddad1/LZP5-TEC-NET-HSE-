ADNOC HSE Standards Framework

Minimum HSE Requirements

Always wear Your PPE :

Helmets, gloves, glasses, and hi-vis gear—no shortcuts; it's your first line of defence on site.

Safe Work Practices :

Do Job Safety Analysis (JSA) before starting, use tools right, and never work alone in risky spots.

Emergency Response :

Know your muster point, fire exits, and alarms—practice drills and report injuries or spills right away.

Housekeeping & Environment :

Keep areas clean and tidy and watch for spills to protect our team and planet.

Implementation and Compliance

Daily Toolbox Talks :

Quick team huddles to refresh rules and share tips—everyone joins in to stay sharp and ready.

On-Site Checks :

Supervisors and HSE officers watch for unsafe acts and unsafe conditions and fix them fast, no delays.

Consequences for Non-Compliance :

Skipping rules means stops, retraining, or removal—safety first, always fair.

ADNOC Life Saving Rules ‘LSR’

WORK AUTHORIZATION

Always obtain, understand, and comply with a valid work permit before starting any work.

CONFINED SPACE

Enter only when you are trained, authorized, and all safety controls are in place.

ENERGY ISOLATION

Confirm that all energy sources are isolated, locked, and tagged out before work begins ‘LOTO’.

BYPASSING SAFETY CONTROL

Never disable alarms, interlocks, or safety devices without proper approval.

WORKING AT HEIGHT

Always use a full-body harness and fall protection when there is a risk of falling.

SAFE MECHANICAL LIFTING

Make sure lifting operations are planned, controlled, and supervised by competent personnel.

TOXIC GAS

Ensure the atmosphere is tested and monitored for safe oxygen levels and absence of harmful gases before and during work.

An example of toxic gases in the oil and gas industry is Hydrogen Sulfide (H2S ) Hydrogen Sulfide is a colorless, flammable gas known for its foul odor of rotten eggs. It is highly toxic and can pose serious health risks to workers if inhaled in high concentrations. (H2S ) is commonly encountered in oil and gas extraction, refining, and processing operations, especially in sour gas reservoirs. Due to its toxicity and corrosiveness, it requires strict safety measures and detection systems to protect personnel and infrastructure.

DRIVING

1. Always wear a seatbelt, obey traffic laws, and stay focused on driving.
2. While driving, do not use your phone and do not exceed speed limits

LINE OF FIRE

Keep yourself & others out of the line of fire

HOT WORK

Control flammable & ignition sources

ADNOC Work Management System ‘WMS’

TARGET

“A safety tool to ensure that the work is properly controlled, coordinated and communicated”

Purpose

The Objectives :

1. Description of key aspects and requirements
2. Facilitate harmonization and integration
3. Defines the structure of Work Management System
4. Provides requirements of WMS elements

PERMIT TO WORK ‘PTW’

Can be described as …

1. Work (other than normal Operations)
2. Activity that comprises one or more tasks
3. Work that requires agreement & planning
4. Work that requires authorization and management of work related and other related risk involved

System containing documents for controlling and coordinating work to:

1. Establish and maintain safe working conditions
2. Ensure hazards & controls are defined and implemented
3. Act as a written agreement between parties

The Permit clearly documents:

1. Specific work scope;
2. Known worksite conditions via supporting documents;
3. Preparations, worksite hazards, precautions and controls
4. Equipment, systems, buildings and work areas associated
5. Affected parties and required communications
6. Requirements for personal protective equipment
7. Verification of preparations and controls
8. Revalidation

Purpose

Ensure that a well-defined system is implemented across all ADNOC sites ensuring that:

1. Work sites and equipment are adequately prepared
2. The personnel executing the work fully understand scope
3. Work activities are carried out safely aligned to conditions
4. Worksite is returned to a safe condition
5. A time validity is applied to each Permit
6. A formal hand-back procedure is in place
7. Monitoring is carried out
8. PTW exemptions are identified, assessed and approved

Types Of PTW:-

Critical Work Permit :

1. A permit used for critical work activities listed in ‘Work Categorization Chart’ or identified by the Group Company as Critical or High/ High-Medium Risk (as per ADNOC RAM).
2. Spark Potential nature of work will be covered under the Critical Permit.

Hot Work Permit

1. A permit used for activities that contain a positive source of ignition as part of the nature of work activity. Examples include but not limited to hot surfaces, naked flames, hot gases, mechanically produced sparks, chemical reactions, etc.
2. Any confirmed source of ignition shall require use of the Hot Work Permit.

General Work Permit

A Permit used for activities that does not form part of the defined critical work, and does not contain any hot work.

Certificates

1.Set of supporting documents (Shall be attached to PTW)
2. Complementary to PTWs (Do not in themselves, authorize work to be carried out)
3. Standalone documents (Assist in identifying & certifying controls, prerequisite and conditions req prior to permit issue)
4. Used for preparatory work (for the main permit)

Purpose

The Objectives :

1. Description of key aspects and requirements
2. Facilitate harmonization and integration
3. Defines the structure of Work Management System
4. Provides requirements of WMS elements

Confined Space Entry Certificate

Any work that requires entry into a confined space or into an area that could become a confined space through a fall in oxygen levels through introduction of harmful airborne contaminants or presents difficulty in rescuing and treating an injured or unconscious person.

Excavation Certificate

1. Required whenever breaking into ground (including drilling) and cutting into floors, walls, or ceilings to specify the precautions needed to protect personnel against collapse of the excavation or the hazards of penetrating/damaging underground cables, pipes or services during excavation work.
2. Note: Excavation Certificate is applicable in all areas (Restricted/Unrestricted)
3. Authorized Civil Personnel Responsible for all excavations onsite and the authorizing signatory for Excavation Certificates

Vehicle & Machine Entry Certificate

1. Required whenever operating equipment with combustion engines such as forklift trucks, generators, compressors, etc. are introduced into Zone Classified Hazardous areas.
2. Vehicle and Machine Entry Certificate is valid for one vehicle. Any change in vehicle requires approval from Area Authority (AA)

Diving Certificate

1. A Diving Certificate is required to ensure that appropriate control measures are strictly applied to all activities involving a diving operation which reduce the risks to a diving team to ALARP.
2. Note: Diving Certificate shall be issued to Diving Supervisors only

Ionizing Radiation Certificate

Required for all activities that involve handling and/or use of radioactive sources such as Radiography and Radioactivity based process instruments and NORM contaminated waste from process lines/vessels/tanks.

Atmospheric Test Record Form

1. In order to determine the concentration of harmful vapors and/or the adequacy of the oxygen content, periodic or continuous atmospheric testing during the work would be required.
2. Note : All Efforts shall be made to conduct Atmospheric test within 1 hour to the commencement of work

Easement Certificate

Required for any construction and/or repair activities performed in the common service/ utility area with pipelines/ communication/ electrical power lines.

Temporary Defeat Certificate

Any work activity that requires the temporary bypassing of a safety system including over-ride, defeat or inhibition of function on equipment identified as HSE Safety Critical Equipment and Systems (HSECES).

Road Closure Certificate

1. Required for work activities that require closure of roads.
2. Max Validity of 28 Calendar Days.

Energy Isolation Certificate – Mechanical. Process, Instrumentation

Work that requires isolating the energy source/equipment to be able to conduct the work activity.

Energy Isolation Certificate – Electrical

Work that requires isolating the energy source/equipment to be able to conduct the work activity.
